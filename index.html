Key Principles

Groove is everything in your genre. Use Musicca Metronome free in your browser.

Drums
Your level: Early Beginner  ·  Gear: Full Kit  ·  Focus: Groove, pocket + locking with guitar
01
Since you have a real kit, your #1 priority is a solid, consistent 4/4 beat: kick on 1 and 3, snare on 2 and 4, hi-hat keeping steady 8th notes. Get that completely automatic before adding anything. Use a metronome every single session.
02
The most powerful thing you can do early: record your guitar, then sit behind the kit and play a beat over it. This is literally how songs get made. It builds groove instinct, coordination, and your ear for how drums and guitar lock together.
03
For groovy genres, study the "pocket." Listen to how the kick and snare breathe slightly behind the beat in funk and soul. Clyde Stubblefield (James Brown), Questlove, and John Bonham are your reference points. Copy their feel, not just their patterns.
04
Beginner milestone goal: learn a basic rock beat, a basic shuffle (triplet feel), and a simple syncopated funk groove. Those three patterns cover most of the music you want to play and write.
05
Practice pad work still matters even with a full kit. Use it for rudiments (single stroke, double stroke, paradiddle) during low-noise times. 10 minutes of rudiment work a day will rapidly improve your control and speed on the kit.
🎹
Piano
Your level: Early Beginner  ·  Gear: Real Piano  ·  Focus: Theory, chords + songwriting
01
Having a real piano is a massive advantage for theory. The visual layout makes keys, scales, and chord shapes click in a way a fretboard never does. Spend even 10-15 min a week here and your guitar theory understanding will double in speed.
02
First goal: play the C major scale with both hands, then build the I-IV-V-vi chords (C, F, G, Am) in root position. Once those are solid, transpose to G major. This teaches you everything about how keys work.
03
Use piano as a songwriting sketch pad. When you have a chord progression idea, play it on piano first. You can hear the harmony more cleanly than on guitar.
04
For groovy genres, learn to play basic 7th chords on piano: Cmaj7, Am7, Dm7, G7. These are the building blocks of soul, R&B, and funk harmony.
05
Free lessons: Playground Sessions free tier or Simply Piano's free content are great structured options. Pair with musictheory.net so you can immediately apply what you read to real keys.
